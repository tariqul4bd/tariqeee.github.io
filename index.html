Global Entrepreneurship Summer Camp Program 2019 Universiti Sains Islam Malaysia (USIM), Malaysia

Organized by: "Universiti Sains Islam Malaysia (USIM)" and "Daffodil International University (DIU) "
In the summer of 2019, I had the distinguished opportunity to participate in the "Global Entrepreneurship Summer Camp Program" held at "Universiti Sains Islam Malaysia (USIM)" , a leading institution known for fostering entrepreneurial skills among students worldwide. This intensive program brought together 60 students from various international backgrounds to engage in rigorous workshops focusing on business idea generation and product development.

Over the course of the program, we were tasked with designing innovative business models and developing viable products that addressed real-world problems. Collaborating in diverse teams, we applied theoretical knowledge in practical scenarios, undergoing a series of critiques and iterations to refine our proposals.

My contribution to our team was instrumental in us achieving a remarkable second place in the competition. Our project was recognized for its creativity, feasibility, and potential for positive impact, standing out among many impressive contenders. This achievement was a testament to our strategic thinking, teamwork, and adaptability in a competitive and multicultural environment.

As a recognition of our exceptional performance and the skills demonstrated during the program, USIM awarded each member of our team a certificate worth three academic credits. This accolade not only highlighted our practical achievements but also contributed significantly to our academic profiles, underscoring our capabilities in entrepreneurship and innovation.

This experience was pivotal in my educational journey, enriching my understanding of global business challenges and enhancing my entrepreneurial skills. It provided a platform to network with like-minded peers and industry professionals, broadening my perspective on international business dynamics and cultural diversity.
